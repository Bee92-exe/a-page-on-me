English Poems

Words written in silence,
where loneliness learns to speak.

A Ghost Named Love

I still remember the shade you loved the most;
From a quiet corner, I watched the dress you wore.
I still breath the faint fragnance of your hair,
Like spring that lingers softly in despair.

Your gentle eyes - two stars of calm,
Drew my soul with their fevered charm.
I keep the handkerchief once entwined,
It's tender line recalls your mind.

Your lips - so soft, so shy, so fair;
Held a kiss I still find everywhere.
The mirror forgets my face, not your trace;
I live in rooms still haunted by your grace.

The letters fade, the ink grows pale;
Yet memory sails where time can't sail.
The wind still carries your laughter ghost,
The moonlight bends to where you stood most.

The evening hums our broken tune,
The stars recall that vanished june.
I whisper your name into empty prayer,
And feel you breathing though you're not here.

I don't seek you to return,
Nor ask the past again to burn.
I only wish the night could know,
How much I loved you, long ago.

Now let me fade where echos sleep,
In dreams your silent memories keep.
For some love's end, but our became
A Ghost that whispers through your name.

Empty Rooms

I learned silence from empty rooms,
where echoes knew my name better than people.

Love arrived quietly,
like dust on unused furniture,
and left the same way.

The Ghost I Married

I still write to you,
as if words could reach wherever you went.
Not Heaven,
not another world—
just… somewhere that I can no longer enter,
though I once lived in the warmth of your eyes.

You weren’t just a girl.
You were a season.
A whole sky I learned to breathe under,
a silence I once wore like a second skin.

The day you left,
the world didn’t end.
It slowed.
It blurred.
Like someone took the sun and dimmed it,
but left just enough light to make me remember
what I lost.

I still see you.
in the corners of quiet afternoons,
in the threads of your old voice notes
I no longer play
but never delete.
You are not gone.
You are diffused—
into everything that hurts quietly now.

Sometimes I look at my reflection
and wonder when my eyes stopped belonging to me.
When did they start searching for you?
They still look for you.
Still wait for you to walk back into frame,
like a movie that never ends,
just pauses forever.

And if someone asks me why I still carry you,
I tell them this,
Because some loves
do not die,
they become ghosts—
and I married mine.

I still set a place for you
in my thoughts.
I still speak your name
into poems I never share.
I still live with the absence of your presence
like a song that plays only in dreams.

I don’t ask you to come back.
I don’t beg the past.
I just… remember.
And remembering you feels holy,
even if it hurts.

You were the only thing that ever made the pain feel
like it had purpose.

So no,
I don’t want to forget.
I want to live like a shrine
to the love that never had an ending.
I want to be the boy
who once loved a girl
so much,
he made time kneel
just to remember her.

The Daylight Where You Linger

I saw your face today,
not in a dream,
but in the quiet curve of morning light
spilling across the floor—
the kind of light that doesn't ask for attention,
just quietly stays.

You looked the same.
Hair resting on your shoulder like it always did,
eyes full of stories you never told me
but I somehow understood anyway.

You didn’t speak,
and neither did I.
We never needed words, remember?

I wanted to reach for you—
not to hold,
not to bring you back,
but just to feel
what it was like to stand in your presence again
without the ache.

You were once my entire sky.
Now you’re the soft ache in the wind,
the familiar sting behind closed eyes,
the silence after a favorite song ends.

And still,
I wouldn’t trade it—
even this pain.
Even the nights it tears me apart.

Because loving you,
even in your absence,
taught me how deep a heart can feel.
How one smile can live forever
inside someone who never got to say goodbye.

So I write this,
not to bring you back,
but to say:
You were real.
You mattered.
And you still live here—
in the quiet places of me
that no one else will ever reach.

Philosophy of Waiting

I wait, not for people,
but for meanings to arrive.

Some never do.
Some arrive too late,
asking why I’m no longer the same.

A Letter I Never Sent

Hey, I don’t know where to begin,
Maybe from that moment you smiled and unknowingly rewrote my world.
You were poetry before I even knew how to read feelings.
You walked in -
not with fireworks, but with silence that felt like home.
Back then,
everything felt light.
The sky had your name in its shade of blue.
And my heart;
God, it wasn’t just beating, it was singing.
We never needed to say it out loud, did we?
It was in the pauses.
In the way I waited for your voice
like the moon waits for the tide.
But I never told you. how sacred your presence was.
How you made me feel seen
as if someone finally knew the language of my silence.
And maybe that’s where I failed.
I loved you in silence,
when I should’ve screamed it into the wind.
Then came the slow fading.
No fight. No goodbye. Just… distance.
Like watching a star burn out in the sky
but pretending the night is still the same.
I still don’t know what we were,
but I know what I was—
in love.

Now?
Now I live with ghosts.
You're everywhere and nowhere.
In every poem I can't finish.
In every song I skip because it sounds too much like your name.
And sometimes, when I laugh,
I wonder if it would’ve sounded better with you beside me.
And still the truth remains,
not spoken, but aching in verse—
I never stopped carrying you.
You live in the softest parts of me,
in the tears I hide,
in the dreams I never speak of.
I don't want to move on.
Not because I can't—
but because this love,
even in its ache,
is mine.
It made me who I am.
And letting it go would mean letting go of a piece of my soul.
So wherever you are.
if you ever wonder—
Yes, someone once loved you like the sky loves stars,
even if he never said it in time.
And he still does.
Quietly.
Always.

Love, in Lowercase

Love doesn’t scream here.
It whispers, fails, returns,
and learns how to stay quiet.

What they DID!

I was soft once.
Then the world came,
with its sharp hands
and its crooked gods
and tore pages out of me
until I forgot how the story ends.
